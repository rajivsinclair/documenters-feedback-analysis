Documenters Checklist Feedback Analysis

Cluster analysis of 4,926 feedback notes from Documenters' checklist submissions

About This Analysis

During our brainstorming session with the Docs HQ team we explored ideas for expanding the Contextualizer concept for Documenters. One idea that emerged was adding a field to the checklist for Documenters to "leave a note" for the next Documenter that will cover a meeting from that agency. Before lean testing this concept by adding or editing a question to the checklist, we wanted to analyze the data currently coming in from the checklist question "Any feedback for us that will help us better support Documenters on a future assignment like this?". Specifically, we wanted to understand how often this optional checklist field is being used, along with analyzing the content that Documenters are currently sharing in this field.

This analysis uses AI to identify patterns and themes across 4, 926 feedback entries, revealing insights about democratic participation, meeting accessibility, and civic engagement challenges across American cities.

The Feedback Checklist Field

This feedback field is included at the end of the checklists that Documenters complete during or after their assignment.

Identified Feedback Themes

Process & Learning Feedback (724 entries - 14.7%)
This cluster captures documenters sharing their learning process, first-time experiences, and feedback about the documentation toolkit and process itself. These entries focus on self-reflection, procedural suggestions, and personal experiences navigating assignments. Documenters frequently offer constructive feedback about the tools, training materials, and processes that could improve their experience and effectiveness.
Key Insight: New documenters need better onboarding resources and experienced documenters are actively sharing knowledge about best practices and procedural improvements. This represents a valuable knowledge-sharing opportunity that could be systematized.
Representative Examples:
1. "I recommend links to the facilities (Dickerson, JDF, etc.); a legal glossary; and a review of the last proceedings to help new Documenters understand the context and terminology used in these meetings."
2. "Had a great time doing this assignment. Toolkit contents were useful, and directions were easy to understand. The pre-meeting research suggestions helped me prepare effectively."
3. "Thank you for the opportunity as this is my first paid Documenters assignment. I welcome any and all feedback on my performance and documentation approach."
4. "I think a script that would help people understand the direction of the project would help us help you document more effectively and ask better follow-up questions."
5. "It would be great if there were a way to upload multiple files at a time (takes awhile to upload all the screenshots) and perhaps a batch processing feature for efficiency."
Meeting Access & Organization Issues (1,519 entries - 30.8%)
This cluster focuses on barriers to accessing and following meetings, including missing agendas, poor audio quality, inability to identify speakers, and general organizational problems that prevent effective documentation and public participation. These issues represent fundamental challenges in democratic transparency and civic engagement that affect both documenters and the general public's ability to meaningfully participate in government proceedings.
Key Insight: Meeting organizers need standardized protocols for agenda distribution, speaker identification, and audio quality to ensure both documenters and the public can effectively follow proceedings. Poor organization undermines democratic participation and wastes community resources.
Representative Examples:
1. "Very few copies of physical agendas were provided at the meeting, but having a place to find this online beforehand would be beneficial for both preparation and real-time reference."
2. "The audio for this meeting was terrible. The only person I could hear adequately most of the time was the Chair. Other speakers were completely inaudible, making documentation nearly impossible."
3. "I couldn't find a list of members of this board anywhere. One of the speakers was listed as 'landmarks' - are these always the same people? Who are they? Why is their website so unhelpful?"
4. "There was no meeting agenda made available to the public. Was unable to identify speakers in the meeting apart from the presenters, making it difficult to provide context for their statements."
5. "Try to figure out how to hear and see all of the Commissioners. Due to the way they are set up it is difficult to see individuals names and impossible to hear some of them clearly."
Community Engagement & Civic Process (1,456 entries - 29.6%)
This cluster emphasizes community participation, public discourse, and the civic engagement aspects of meetings. Documenters describe community attendance, public comment effectiveness, and how well meetings serve their democratic function of public participation. These observations provide valuable insights into the health of democratic processes and the effectiveness of civic engagement mechanisms in different cities and contexts.
Key Insight: Documenters observe significant community engagement challenges and opportunities, noting when public participation is effective versus when procedural barriers limit civic engagement. Their observations could inform best practices for facilitating meaningful public participation across different governmental bodies.
Representative Examples:
1. "This was a great event. I would say about 75-85 people were in attendance and were attentive to what the candidates had to say. The format allowed for meaningful community interaction."
2. "This assignment was truly eye opening on Public Discourse of Community Engagement in Planning, TIFs, Developments in Chicago. The level of community knowledge and passion was remarkable."
3. "Although commissioners and some in public attendance had very strong disagreements, things remained civil and all agenda items were addressed with respectful discourse."
4. "They seemed to be able to address most public comment concerns either immediately or advising of a follow up, which showed genuine engagement with community input."
5. "Before the meeting, I found it helpful to look on the Board's Web page at 'Cases Currently Before the Police Board' to understand the context and ongoing community concerns."
Technical Infrastructure & Virtual Meetings (1,227 entries - 24.9%)
This cluster addresses technical challenges with virtual and hybrid meetings, including streaming issues, audio/video problems, recording difficulties, and the logistics of documenting meetings that blend in-person and virtual participation. As government meetings increasingly adopt hybrid formats, these technical infrastructure challenges have become critical barriers to public participation and effective documentation.
Key Insight: Virtual and hybrid meeting infrastructure requires significant improvement, with consistent audio/video quality and reliable streaming being essential for effective documentation. Poor technical infrastructure excludes community members and undermines the democratic promise of increased accessibility through technology.
Representative Examples:
1. "This was a hybrid meeting so some members were in person and others on Microsoft Teams. Not everyone was clearly identifiable or audible the entire time, creating confusion about who was speaking."
2. "I had trouble with Vimeo livestream page - could not consistently get both audio and video, and I'm tech savvy enough. Regular community members would be completely unable to participate."
3. "My sound went out about 1:34 started back up with sound at 1:49 and that was the end of the meeting. Missing critical portions due to technical failures is deeply frustrating."
4. "Unlike regular board meetings, this one only featured the shared screen--no view of the room or participants. Kind of weird and made it impossible to read body language or engagement."
5. "The audio quality coming through was pretty bad, so I'm not entirely sure what to do in that type of situation. Should I interrupt? Call in? Switch platforms? Guidance needed."

Methodology

1
Data Collection
Extracted 4,926 feedback entries from the Documenters database, filtering for substantial content (>50 characters).
PostgreSQL + Python
2
AI Embeddings
Used Google's gemini-embedding-001 model to convert feedback text into high-dimensional vectors.
Gemini Embeddings
3
Clustering
Applied KMeans clustering with k=4 after testing multiple approaches to find balanced, meaningful clusters.
KMeans + UMAP
4
Theme Analysis
Generated cluster descriptions using Google's Gemini-2.5-flash model to identify distinct themes.
Gemini 2.5 Flash
